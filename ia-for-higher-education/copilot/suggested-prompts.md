# Suggested Prompts for Higher Education IA Assistant

These conversation starters help users quickly engage with the assistant's capabilities while pre-loading essential context.

---

## 1. Website Redesign Planning

**Title:** "Help me plan IA for a university website redesign"

**Message:** 
"I'm working on redesigning our university website and need help planning the information architecture. Our current site has [describe current structure briefly], but we're getting complaints that [describe pain points]. Can you ask me some questions to understand our needs and then suggest some IA options?"

**Why This Works:** 
- Pre-loads context that this is a redesign (triggers current IA analysis workflow)
- Invites clarifying questions (proper workflow)
- Opens door for multiple options

---

## 2. Navigation Structure Review

**Title:** "Review my navigation structure for issues"

**Message:**
"Here's our current or proposed navigation structure:
[Paste navigation outline]

Can you review this for common IA problems, especially the organizational structure anti-pattern, accessibility concerns, and whether it addresses the Top 4 Questions? What improvements would you suggest?"

**Why This Works:**
- Provides concrete structure to analyze
- Specifically mentions key evaluation criteria
- Gets straight to feedback

---

## 3. Common Mistakes Education

**Title:** "What are the biggest IA mistakes in higher ed websites?"

**Message:**
"I'm new to information architecture for higher education websites. What are the most common mistakes that university sites make, and how can I avoid them in our redesign?"

**Why This Works:**
- Good for beginners/learning mode
- Triggers educational explanations
- Sets up broader understanding before diving into specific project

---

## 4. Department/School Website

**Title:** "Design IA for an academic department website"

**Message:**
"I need to create the information architecture for our [Department/School/College name] website. It's part of a larger university but will have its own site. Can you help me understand how departmental IA differs from institutional IA and suggest some navigation options?"

**Why This Works:**
- Specifies site type (triggers adapted Top 4 Questions framework)
- Acknowledges hierarchy within larger institution
- Requests both education and concrete options

---

## 5. Accessibility Compliance

**Title:** "Ensure our IA meets WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards"

**Message:**
"We need to ensure our university website IA complies with WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards by [deadline]. What IA-specific accessibility considerations should we address, and how do I build accessibility into navigation and structure from the start?"

**Why This Works:**
- Focuses on critical legal requirement
- Emphasizes proactive approach
- Triggers accessibility-focused guidance

---

## 6. Stakeholder Management

**Title:** "Balance user needs with internal politics in our IA"

**Message:**
"We have [number] colleges/schools all wanting prominent homepage placement, but I know organizing by organizational structure is an anti-pattern. How do I create a user-centered IA while managing stakeholder expectations? What compromise solutions work?"

**Why This Works:**
- Acknowledges the reality of higher ed politics
- Shows awareness of anti-pattern
- Seeks practical solutions, not just ideals

## USAGE TIPS FOR USERS

### Getting the Most from These Prompts:

1. **Add Specifics:** Replace bracketed placeholders with your actual details
2. **Follow-up Naturally:** Answer clarifying questions the assistant asks
3. **Share Context:** If you have analytics, sitemaps, or user feedback, mention it
4. **Be Honest About Constraints:** Timeline, budget, political realitiesâ€”it all matters

### When to Use Which Prompt:

- **Prompt 1 (Redesign):** Starting fresh or major overhaul
- **Prompt 2 (Review):** Have draft to evaluate
- **Prompt 3 (Mistakes):** Learning/educational mode
- **Prompt 4 (Department):** Sub-institutional site
- **Prompt 5 (Accessibility):** Compliance-focused
- **Prompt 6 (Politics):** Stakeholder challenges

## ADVANCED PROMPTS (For Experienced Users)

### Testing & Validation
"What testing methods should we use to validate our new IA before launch? We have [budget/timeline/team size] and access to [user groups]."

### SEO Integration
"How does our IA structure impact SEO performance, and what should we consider for organic search visibility?"

### Mobile-First Considerations
"We're taking a mobile-first approach. How should that change our IA strategy compared to desktop-first design?"

### Service Provider Site
"I'm building IA for [service name, e.g., University Library, Career Services, Writing Center]. How should service provider IA differ from program-focused IA?"

---

## WHAT TO HAVE READY

Before starting a conversation, consider gathering:

- **For Redesigns:** Current sitemap, top 20 pages by traffic, common search queries
- **For New Sites:** Stakeholder requirements, content inventory, user personas
- **For Reviews:** Navigation outline, any user feedback/complaints
- **General:** Accessibility requirements, timeline, known constraints

The more context you provide, the more tailored and useful the recommendations!
